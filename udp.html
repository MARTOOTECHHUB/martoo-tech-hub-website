Understanding UDP

User Datagram Protocol (UDP) is a communication protocol used for time-sensitive transmissions, such as video streaming or online gaming, where speed is more critical than reliability.

Key Features of UDP

  • Low latency, ideal for real-time applications
  • No connection establishment, reducing overhead
  • Simple and efficient for unicast, multicast, and broadcast transmissions

How to Use UDP

To utilize UDP, you need to configure your applications to send and receive data packets without establishing a connection. Consult our resources for specific implementation details.
